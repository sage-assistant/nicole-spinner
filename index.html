Episodes

1
IVEY / MCR
▶
The Origin Protocol 52m

A young Nicole Spinner enrolls at Ivey Business School. Within months, professors begin noticing something unusual: she actually understands marketing. Not theoretically. Viscerally. A semester at Manchester Business School in the UK raises further questions among international intelligence agencies. Was she recruited? Trained? Or was she simply born this way? Experts disagree. Nicole does not care. She's already moved on.

2
BBDO / TAXI
▶
Inside the Machine 48m

Nicole penetrates the inner circles of Canada's advertising elite. BBDO Worldwide. Juniper Park. TAXI. Red Lion. At each stop, she climbs faster than HR can update the org chart. Account Coordinator to Account Executive to Account Supervisor to Account Director. Four agencies. Each one thought they'd keep her. None did. One former creative director, speaking on condition of anonymity, admits: "She wasn't climbing the ladder. She was the ladder. We were just... leaning on her."

3
WE
▶
The Purpose Pivot 55m

Most people pick a lane. Nicole picks all the lanes and merges them into a highway. As Associate Director of Partner & Product Marketing at WE, she orchestrates brand partnerships across fashion, kids' toys, and food & beverage that simultaneously generate revenue AND help communities in need. "We asked her to choose between profit and purpose," recalls a colleague. "She looked at us like we'd asked her to choose a favorite child. Which, for the record, she also refuses to do. She has three."

4
BEAR CO.
▶
Bear Market 51m

2018. Nicole is at WE, raising three boys, and apparently has enough bandwidth left over to casually found The Bear Co., a custom eco-friendly teething products company. For children. Because the woman whose day already requires military-grade logistics looked at her schedule and thought: "You know what this needs? A product line." Industry analysts attempted to model how this was physically possible. The models broke. They called it "a scheduling anomaly inconsistent with known human time constraints."

5
KIDS&CO
▶
The Corporate Childcare Coup 58m

Nicole joins Kids & Company as Director of Marketing & Brand. Within what Human Resources describes as "an unreasonable timeframe," she's VP. She now oversees marketing, brand, AND the company's entire ESG strategy. That's three departments. Three executive functions. She also has three children. "We're starting to think three is her number," notes a board member. "Three agencies before she stopped counting. Three roles. Three boys. If she gets a fourth of anything we may need to convene an emergency session."
