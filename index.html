The Origin Protocol 52m

A young Nicole Spinner enrolls at Ivey Business School. Within months, professors begin noticing something unusual: she actually understands marketing. A brief but suspicious stint at Manchester Business School in the UK raises questions. Was she recruited? Trained? Or was she always this way?

Inside the Machine 48m

Nicole penetrates the inner circles of Canada's ad elite. BBDO. Juniper Park. TAXI. Red Lion. At each stop, she climbs faster than anyone can explain. Account Coordinator becomes Account Executive becomes Account Supervisor becomes Account Director. Four agencies in what felt like four minutes. Former colleagues describe her trajectory as "honestly kind of alarming."

The WE Years 55m

Nicole pivots to purpose. As Associate Director of Partner & Product Marketing at WE, she orchestrates brand partnerships across fashion, toys, and food that somehow also help communities in need. Colleagues recall a woman who could close a deal with a Fortune 500 brand and design a product for underserved children in the same afternoon. "She made the rest of us look like we were just... sitting there," says one source.

Bear Market 51m

In 2018, Nicole does the unthinkable: she starts The Bear Co. Custom eco-friendly teething products for children. From ad agency boardrooms to baby product R&D. "Most people go from corporate to startup and flame out in six months," notes an industry analyst. "Nicole went from corporate to startup to somehow making sustainable baby products cool. That's not normal behavior."

The Corporate Childcare Coup 58m

Nicole joins Kids & Company as Director of Marketing & Brand. Within what can only be described as "an unreasonable amount of time," she's promoted to VP. She now oversees marketing, brand, AND the company's entire ESG strategy. "That's three jobs," confirms a human resources expert. "She technically has three jobs. We checked."
